Should You Add Face Mask to Your Skincare Routine?


Face mask is known for its therapeutic benefits. The formula comes from clay, honey, and milk that would nourish the skin back in the day. Around the 1900s, face masks became skin treatments for people suffering from eczema and acne. Today, a face mask is used for multiple reasons. Some people want a deep cleanse, while some want to exfoliate and hydrate weekly. Face masks are also known to give a relaxing weekend too. So, the answer to your question depends on the results you are looking for because face masks have many benefits.

Who Should Use a Face Mask?


Do you have a 3-step skincare routine? Add the 4th step to it and change your life – use face masks. Other than for pampering yourself, you should also use face masks:

  •  If you have oily skin, face masks can help remove oil and avoid breakouts.
  •  For dry skin types, masks can deep moisturize your skin and leave you without patchy skin.
  •  Those of you with sensitive skin types, it can soothe irritation, sun burn, inflammation, and sun damage.


If you can relate to any of the above, Allure Beauty will sort your life out. We want you to have the best skin, which is why we bring you a wide range of face masks. From ones that plump your skin to soothing mask treatments like peel-offs. But in case you are not sure about which one to use, we’ll guide you there too!

Adding Face Masks to Your Routine


Now arrives the concern; how do I even make this a part of my skincare routine? It is simple! The first thing you need to do is to cleanse your face with your regular facewash, this removes any impurities that may be resting on your skin.

Once dry, it would be advisable to apply a small patch of the mask on the skin to check for any allergies. As all is good and you have no reaction, carry on with applying the face mask on your skin and leave it on for 20-30 minutes. This is the best part because you get to close your eyes and relax. Once the timer rings, wash off the mask and see the plump difference for yourself!
