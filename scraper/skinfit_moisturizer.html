Moisturizers in Pakistan


An integral part of any skincare routine, moisturizers keep skin supple and protected from environmental aggressors. They make the skin look healthier and more radiant by retaining moisture and warding against dryness. 
 

Types of Moisturizers 


The following are some of the many types of moisturizers available, each with its own set of pros and cons: 
 

Creams:  


These create an occlusive barrier on top of the skin, making it difficult for water to escape. They are thick and substantial. For really dry skin, creams are the way to go. Estee Lauder's Revitalizing Supreme Global Anti-Aging Cell Power Creme and Bee Happy Honey Moisturizer are two examples. 
 

Lotions:  


These absorb faster and are less heavy than creams. Lotions, such as CeraVe's Ultra-Light Moisturizing Lotion and Beauty of Joseon's Dynasty Cream, are great choices for normal, combined, and oily skin. 
 

Gels:  


These feel even more cooling on the skin than lotions do, and they're much lighter. Products like The Ordinary's Natural Moisturizing Factors+HA and Isntree's Hyaluronic Acid Watery Sun Gel are great options for oily, acne-prone skin. 
 

Oils:  


These oils are all-natural and have the ability to moisturize the skin profoundly. On the flip side, they have the potential to be oily and comedogenic, which means they can clog pores. Oils, such as Sulwhasoo's Concentrated Ginseng Renewing Cream, are ideal for older, dry skin. 
 

Benefits of Moisturizers 


Your skin will reap many benefits from using a moisturizer on a daily basis: 
 

Hydration:  


Moisturizers are great for maintaining healthy, plump skin since they both hydrate the skin and keep it that way. Because older and dry skin typically produces less oil, this is particularly crucial for them. 
 

Protection:  


By forming an outer layer on top of the skin, they shield it from harmful elements like pollution, dry air, and even ultraviolet radiation (if the moisturizer has an SPF). 
 

Improved texture:  


Moisturizers, when used on a regular basis, can soften rough skin, diminish the look of fine lines and wrinkles, and make the skin look younger. Embryolisse Lait-Creme Concentre and L'Oréal Paris Hyaluron Expert Night Cream are two examples of products developed with these issues in mind. 
 

Enhanced barrier function:  


Maintaining a healthy moisture barrier is essential for keeping the skin free from irritating inflammation, as well as protecting it from allergens and harmful microorganisms. For example, the Toleriane Double Repair Face Moisturizer by La Roche-Posay contains ceramides, which the skin can use to improve its barrier function. 
 

Ingredients 


There are a number of components in moisturizers that serve to both hydrate and protect the skin. Here are a few typical ingredients: 
 

Hyaluronic acid:  


Isntree Hyaluronic Acid Watery Sun Gel and Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel Cream are just two of the many moisturizers that use this humectant because of its ability to draw in and hold onto skin moisture. 
 

Glycerin:  


Here, we have another humectant that penetrates the skin and pulls moisture from the air. 
 

Ceramides:  


The skin's barrier function is supported by these lipids, which are present in the skin naturally. People whose skin's protective barrier is weak may benefit greatly from ceramide-containing products, such as CeraVe Facial Moisturizing Lotion. 
 

Occlusive ingredients:  


Some examples of these include petrolatum and dimethicone, which act as a physical barrier to keep the skin from losing its natural moisture. One product that has occlusive components is Bee Happy Honey Multi-Purpose Healing Balm. 
 

Emollients:  


Shea butter, cocoa butter, and similar compounds smooth and moisturize the skin. One product that contains emollients is Bobbi Brown Extra Eye Repair Cream. 
 

How to Use Moisturizers 


If you want your moisturizer to work as advertised, do the following: 
 

Cleanse your face:  


Get your face free of grime, sebum, and makeup before you begin. 

After cleansing but before moisturizing, add a toner if desired. 
 

Apply moisturizer:  


Use a small quantity of moisturizer, about the size of a pea, and gently massage it into your face and neck until it absorbs.  

Apply a specialized eye cream, such as Bobbi Brown's Extra Eye Repair Cream, on the sensitive eye area. 
 

Apply sunscreen (daytime):  


Always use sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher after applying a moisturizer during the day.  

Get a moisturizer like CeraVe Facial Moisturizing Lotion or Isntree Hyaluronic Acid Watery Sun Gel that has an SPF already blended into it. 
 

Choosing the Right Moisturizer 


Several factors determine whether moisturizer is ideal for you: 
 

Skin conditions:  


Try to find items that are made especially for skin disorders like rosacea, eczema, or psoriasis. It may be good to use a moisturizer that contains ceramides or niacinamide, which are calming and anti-inflammatory substances. If you have dry or rough skin and are looking for a specialized moisturizer that will help hydrate your skin, try Dry And Rough Skin Solution 10% Urea Moisturizer. 
 

Skin areas:   


It may be helpful to use a separate moisturizer on various areas of your body. You could choose to use a thinner, more moisturizing lotion on your body and a heavier, more SPF-protective moisturizer on your face. Any part of your body can benefit from using The Ordinary's Natural Moisturizing Factors + HA. 
 

Seasons:  


In warmer months, you might want a thinner moisturizer, while in colder months, you might want something thicker. It may be best to use a hyaluronic acid gel that is water-based throughout the summer, such as Isntree Hyaluronic Acid Watery Sun Gel. Meanwhile, the cooler weather calls for a heavier cream, such as Revitalizing Supreme Global Anti Aging Cell Power Creme from Estee Lauder. 
 

Ages:  


Dryness and a decrease in collagen production are signs of aging skin. To combat the signs of aging, seek out a moisturizer that has retinol, peptides, and antioxidants. Skin tone, texture, and the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles can all be improved with the use of these products, which also increase collagen formation. For older skin types, try Sulwhasoo's Concentrated Ginseng Renewing Cream. 
 

Genders:   


The skin of men and women differs in certain ways. Yet, the vast majority of moisturizers are effective on both sexes. Moisturizers are great for many skin types, so it's important to choose one that works for you. 
 

Skin tones:  


Moisturizers are necessary for all skin tones. You can brighten and preserve your skin using products that include antioxidants, such as vitamins C and E. These advantages make the Paula's Choice Probiotic Nutrient Moisturizer a versatile product that works well with a wide range of skin tones. 
 

Skin textures:  


In order to gradually smooth out rough or bumpy skin, try using products that contain exfoliants such as beta-hydroxy acids (BHAs) or alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs). 
 

Skin temperature:  


You should follow your skin type and any concerns you may have when choosing a moisturizer, even if warm and cold skin types may have distinct needs. 
 

Skin sensitivities:  


Moisturizers containing possible irritants, such as scents, dyes, and harsh chemicals, should be avoided by persons with sensitive skin. Instead, seek out moisturizers with fewer components. Beginning with products that are marked as "hypoallergenic" or "fragrance-free" is a smart move. La Roche-Posay Toleriane Double Repair Face Moisturizer is an example of a moisturizer suitable for sensitive skin. 
 

Additional Tips 


Drink plenty of water: For glowing skin, it's important to drink enough water every day. 

Patch test new products: Prior to applying a new moisturizer to your full face, make sure to test it on a tiny patch of skin. 

Store moisturizers properly: To keep your products' efficacy, store them in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight.
